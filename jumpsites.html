VOC Certified Professional System

Professional line of glazing for kitchens and bathrooms.

Triangle Coatings offers a professional glazing system for countertops, bathtubs, tile, sinks, and toilets, certified to meet VOC requirements in all states. The process is built for professional installers and uses a Primer Coating followed by a final coat that makes existing surfaces look like brand-new fixtures.

This reglazing process helps homeowners save thousands of dollars while still delivering the updated bathroom or kitchen appearance they want.

Two-step system: primer coating plus final White Porcelain glaze.
1

Surface Preparation

The preparation process is the most critical step in refinishing because it ensures the new glaze bonds permanently to the surface. For a professional-grade system, the surface must be chemically clean, mechanically etched, and completely dry.

  1. Deep Cleaning: Remove all soap scum, grease, and oils using heavy-duty industrial cleaners or emulsifiers.
  2. Silicone Removal: Scrape away every trace of old silicone caulk, as glaze will not adhere to it.
  3. Acid Etching: Apply a specialized etching compound to porcelain, ceramic, or vitreous china to microscopically roughen the surface.
  4. Sanding: Sand fiberglass, acrylic, or laminate surfaces to create a mechanical key for the primer.
  5. Repairing: Fill chips, deep scratches, or cracks with waterproof polyester putty or waterproof filler.
  6. Final Wipe: Clean the entire surface with isopropyl alcohol or acetone to remove dust.
  7. Masking: Tape off surrounding walls, floors, fixtures, and drains to protect against overspray.
  8. Tack Ragging: Wipe the surface one last time with a tack cloth immediately before spraying to catch airborne dust.
2

Mixing & Application

Achieving a factory-like, durable finish requires a precise two-part mixing process. Adhering strictly to the 5-minute induction time is crucial to prevent bubbling, peeling, or uneven curing.

Primer Coat Mix

Ratio: 70% Primer and 30% Reducer.

Application: Spray evenly onto the properly sanded and cleaned surface. Allow the primer coat to dry completely before proceeding.

Final Coat Mix & Induction

Ratio: 50% White Porcelain, 30% Activator, and 20% Reducer.

Induction Period: Combine thoroughly, then let the final mixture sit for exactly 5 minutes so the activator and base chemically bond before applying over the dried primer.

3

Equipment Setup

Professional glaze finishes are best executed using HVLP setups or fine-finish airless sprayers.

  • Graco: Consider fine-finish tips such as the Graco FFLP series or dedicated systems such as the Graco FinishPro for minimal overspray.
  • Titan: Systems such as the Titan CapSpray series are favored for detailed, high-gloss finishes on tubs and fixtures.

Planning a project?

For targeted troubleshooting steps, collect the exact surface material, current condition, sprayer model, installed tip size, and whether spraying will occur indoors or in a dedicated booth.
