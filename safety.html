Universal Safety & Handling Protocols

First Aid Measures

  • Inhalation: Move the individual to fresh air and keep them comfortable for breathing. Seek immediate medical attention if breathing difficulties occur or the person feels unwell. For 249B Activator isocyanate exposure, administer oxygen or artificial respiration as needed; severe asthmatic reactions can be life-threatening.
  • Skin Contact: Remove contaminated clothing and shoes immediately. Wash with soap and lukewarm, gently flowing water for 15 to 20 minutes. For 249B Activator, first wipe product off with a dry towel or absorbent fabric, or use a polyglycol-based cleanser if available, before washing.
  • Eye Contact: Rinse cautiously with lukewarm water for at least 15 to 20 minutes, holding eyelids open. Remove contact lenses if present and easy to do, then continue rinsing. Get immediate medical attention.
  • Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting. Rinse mouth with water. If vomiting occurs naturally, place the person on their side in the recovery position. Immediately call 911 or a POISON CENTER/doctor and transport to the nearest medical facility.

PPE & Exposure Controls

  • Eye Protection: Wear safety glasses with side shields, indirect-vent goggles, or impact and splash-resistant goggles. Use a face shield with goggles if splash hazards exist.
  • Skin & Body: Wear impermeable protective clothing, including long sleeves, pants, and aprons. Use PVC, neoprene, nitrile rubber, or butyl rubber chemical-resistant gloves and replace contaminated gloves immediately.
  • Respiratory: Use a NIOSH-certified organic vapor/particulate respirator when engineering controls do not keep airborne concentrations below limits. For 249B Activator, use a positive-pressure or continuous-flow supplied-air respirator in confined spaces or when airborne isocyanate levels are unknown or above limits.
  • Workplace: Keep emergency showers and eyewash stations available. Use local exhaust ventilation or explosion-proof equipment to control emissions near the source.

Handling & Storage

  • Keep products away from heat, hot surfaces, sparks, open flames, and other ignition sources. Do not smoke in work areas.
  • Ground and bond containers and receiving equipment, and take precautions against static discharge.
  • Avoid breathing dust, fumes, gases, mists, vapors, or sprays.
  • Wash hands thoroughly after handling. Eating, drinking, and smoking in work areas are prohibited. Contaminated clothing must not leave the workplace and must be washed before reuse.
  • Store locked up in a cool, dry, well-ventilated place with containers tightly closed. Keep away from strong oxidizers, strong alkalis, strong acids, water, amines, and copper alloys.

Fire-Fighting Measures

  • Extinguishing Media: Use dry chemical, carbon dioxide (CO2), foam, or water spray/fog for large fires. Do not use high-volume water jet.
  • Specific Hazards: Vapors are heavier than air, may travel to ignition sources, and can flash back. Closed containers may rupture under extreme heat or water contamination. Thermal decomposition may produce carbon monoxide, carbon dioxide, dense black smoke, and hydrogen cyanide.
  • Fire-Fighting Procedures: Firefighters must wear NFPA-compliant structural firefighting protection, positive-pressure SCBA, and full turnout gear. Cool fire-exposed containers with cold-water spray.

Accidental Release & Spill Clean-Up

  • Evacuate non-emergency personnel, isolate the hazard area, eliminate ignition sources, wear required PPE, and avoid walking through spilled material.
  • Prevent material from entering sewers, storm drains, or waterways. Contain using sand, earth, clay, vermiculite, kitty litter, or similar absorbents. Shovel into an approved metal container.
  • 249B Activator: Decontaminate spill surfaces with neutralization solution, such as water, non-ionic surfactant, and isopropanol/ammonia, or commercial stripping solution. Wait 15 minutes after the first application, cover with absorbent, and verify decontamination with a surface wipe test. Do not tighten the waste-container lid immediately because carbon dioxide gas may expand.

Disposal Considerations

  • Dispose of contents and containers in compliance with federal, state, and local environmental laws. Incineration is preferred for certain components.
  • Empty containers retain residue and hazards. Do not heat, cut, glaze, weld, or pressurize empty containers because highly toxic vapors and gases can form.
